A Crisis Hidden
in Plain Sight:
Indonesia's Forests

Indonesia's forests covered over 160 million hectares in 2000 — one of the world's most biodiverse tropical ecosystems. Since then, nearly 32 million hectares have been cleared, making the nation a net carbon source of 356 million Mg CO₂e per year.

Section 03 · Statistical Insights

What the Data Tells Us

Critical
🌲

19.9% Forest Cover Lost

Of 160.6M ha of forest in 2000, 31.96M ha (19.9%) was lost by 2024. The worst single year was 2016 at 2.42M ha — 4.5× the lowest year (2003: 544K ha).

Critical
🌡️

Net Carbon Source: +356M Mg/yr

Gross emissions average 967M Mg CO₂e/yr against removals of only 611M Mg CO₂/yr — a net +356M Mg CO₂e/yr released to the atmosphere from forest disturbance.

Warning
🔥

4 Provinces = 47.5% of National Loss

Riau (4.3M ha), Kalimantan Barat (4.2M ha), Kalimantan Tengah (3.9M ha) and Sumatera Selatan (3.3M ha) together account for 47.5% of all national loss 2001–2024.

Critical
🌊

Riau: 54.8% Extent Lost

Riau has the highest absolute loss at 4.30M ha and the worst loss rate: 54.8% of its 2000 forest extent was cleared in 24 years. Its average annual emissions: 164.5M Mg CO₂e.

Warning
📈

Post-2021 Rebound Underway

Loss dropped to a low of 841K ha in 2021, but rose again to 1.40M ha in 2023 — a 66% jump. This rebound suggests pressure from recent land-use expansion has not yet been contained.

Bright Spot
💚

Papua: Only 2.6% Lost

Papua has lost only 766K ha from a base of 29.6M ha — just 2.6% of its forest cover — and remains a net carbon sink at −39.0M Mg CO₂e/yr. A critical buffer for the global climate.

Top Provincial Emitters

Provinces with the highest tree cover loss are also the dominant carbon emitters. Riau alone emits more than all of Sulawesi combined. Note: some provinces are net carbon sinks despite local loss.
